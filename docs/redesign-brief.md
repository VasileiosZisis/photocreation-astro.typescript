# Redesign Brief

## Visual Thesis

Studio Photo Creation should feel like a private editorial photography atelier:
warm ivory paper, ink-black typography, soft olive shadows, and restrained
champagne details around large photographic moments.

## Content Plan

- Hero: make the studio name unmistakable, with the existing film as the main
  visual anchor and a direct contact action.
- Support: state the photography and video offer with a few confident service
  cues.
- Detail: keep the gallery categories image-led, so visitors can choose a
  celebration type quickly.
- Final CTA: invite visitors to contact the studio without losing the premium
  tone.

## Interaction Thesis

- Use a quiet hero entrance so text and film settle into place.
- Add gentle image lift and desaturation changes on category and gallery hover
  states.
- Use subtle textured backgrounds and anchored editorial shapes to create depth
  without competing with the photography.

## Keep

- Existing logo, routes, manual gallery content, Cloudinary images, PhotoSwipe,
  contact details, address, social profiles, and Google Maps embed.

## Change

- Move from flat white/black blocks to textured ivory and charcoal surfaces.
- Replace oversized utilitarian navigation with a quieter editorial header.
- Give the homepage a real hero, with a single H1 and a clearer contact path.
- Make gallery and contact pages feel related to the homepage.

## Remove

- Heavy black section blocks that overpower the photography.
- Absolute-positioned category layout that is fragile on responsive screens.
- Contact styling that hides important conversion details inside plain rows.
